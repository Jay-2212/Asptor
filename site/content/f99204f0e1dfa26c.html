Union Agriculture Minister says BRICS meeting in Indore to focus on food security, farmer welfare

• THE HINDU NATIONAL • 2026-06-08

A five-day meeting of the BRICS grouping will commence in Indore on Tuesday (June 9, 2026) to discuss food security, smart farming, global agricultural trade and farmer welfare, Union Agriculture Minister Shivraj Singh Chouhan said on Monday (June 8, 2026).

The discussions under India’s presidency will include a three-day agriculture working group meeting from June 9 and a two-day BRICS ministerial meeting from June 12, he said.

BRICS is an intergovernmental organisation comprising eleven major emerging economies: Brazil, Russia, India, China, South Africa, Egypt, Ethiopia, Iran, Saudi Arabia, the United Arab Emirates, and Indonesia.

Highlighting the significance of the meeting, Mr. Chouhan said BRICS is a very strong voice of the developing countries in the world.

“Around half of the world’s population dwells in BRICS countries. It has 42% of the global agricultural land and accounts for more than 42% of the produce. Of the 58 crore farmers across the world, 70% small cultivators live in BRICS nations,” he said.

The Union Minister said the five-day gathering will deliberate on food security, farmer welfare, nutrition, climate-resilient smart agriculture, facilitation of international agricultural trade, supply chains, “digital agriculture”, research, knowledge exchange, artificial intelligence, and robotics.

He said a joint declaration will be issued after consensus among the BRICS Agriculture Ministers. Mr. Chouhan recalled that India has presided over BRICS meetings in 2012, 2016 and 2021.

Last month, Mr. Chouhan had told reporters in Indore that the BRICS meeting, to be held under the leadership of Prime Minister Narendra Modi, would prove to be a historic opportunity for the global agriculture sector. “It will provide a new direction to the world,” he had said.

Coincidentally, the meeting is being held in Madhya Pradesh’s financial capital at a time when the State government has declared 2026 as the Agriculture Welfare Year.
