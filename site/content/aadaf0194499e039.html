    Delhi High Court refuses to entertain PIL seeking NIA probe into CJP's Parliament march

    The Delhi High Court on Friday refused to entertain a PIL seeking a probe by the NIA or any “specialised agency” into the Cockroach Janta Party-led march to Parliament on July 20 to protest against the NEET paper leak and other related issues.

    A bench of Chief Justice D.K. Upadhyaya and Justice Tejas Karia said the alleged incidents of vandalism were in the knowledge and notice of the authorities, who would take action according to the law.

    The bench also said under the law, it was for the Centre to refer any case to the National Investigation Agency (NIA) for investigation, and not the court.

    “If there is an FIR, recourse to the NIA has to be taken by the respective government. How can this be a PIL? You are asking for an opinion that these are fit cases. This job has been entrusted to the central government,” the court remarked while hearing the PIL by Satish Kumar Aggarwal, ex-vice president of the Akhil Bharat Hindu Mahasabha.

    “You approach the authorities; they will take action,” the bench told the petitioner, who also sought identification of “all persons responsible for acts of violence, vandalism, obstruction of emergency services, damage to public and private property and other offences” and registration of an FIR against them.
