    “I want to tell this clearly, there are three demands of the students: The corrupt Education Minister has to go. Second, action on those who thrashed the students, and third, the Prime Minister should apologise to the students,” Rahul Gandhi stated the Opposition’s stance.

    Lok Sabha has to discuss SC Judges Bill: Jagdambika Pal

    Chair Jagdambika Pal reprimanded the Opposition MPs for not allowing proceedings to continue smoothly. He told them that the government has agreed to take up discussion on NEET, but it must be done after being listed in agenda of the House after permission from the Speaker and the Business Advisory Committee.

    He said it is important to take up Rule 377 which permits MPs to raise matters of urgent public importance.

    “Justice delayed is justice denied,” Mr. Pal said, stating that the Lower House is scheduled to take up the Supreme Court (Number of Judges) Amendment Bill, 2026, for consideration and passing.

    Let PM come and make statement in Parliament: Kharge

    LoP in Rajya Sabha Mallikarjun Kharge said that if the Prime Minister has anything to say, he should come and make a statement in Parliament.

    “If he has to say anything, he should say it in Parliament. Only then will we consider his word to be authentic. Let him come and make a statement here. Instead of doing this at 12 am in the night, if he can come and speak here, we will be witnesses to it,” he said.

    ‘Before coming to Parliament, dismiss Dharmendra Pradhan’: Congress to PM Modi

    Congress president Mallikarjun Kharge, addressing Prime Minister Narendra Modi in a X post on Friday (July 24, 2026), said that before coming to Parliament, Mr. Modi must dismiss Dharmendra Pradhan. He said, “Before coming to Parliament today, dismiss Dharmendra Pradhan, apologise to the students and take strict action against those who ordered the use of lathis, batons, and Pellet Guns to suppress the students’ voices.”

    Mr. Kharge stated that the Opposition will be ready to discuss on education system, only after the sacking of Mr. Pradhan.
