Manipur, Meghalaya and Kerala declare holiday on July 20 for students to watch FIFA World Cup final

• THE HINDU NATIONAL • 2026-07-20

GUWAHATI

The governments of Kerala, Meghalaya and Manipur have declared a holiday for all schools and higher educational institutions on Monday (July 20, 2026) to let students watch the FIFA World Cup 2026 final.

Football is the most popular sport in Manipur, which produces an assembly line of footballers representing top clubs across India. Some even play for other States.

The final, between Argentina and Spain, is scheduled to start at 12.30 a.m. IST.

“…the Governor of Manipur is pleased to declare the closure of all schools as may be affiliated to the Board of Secondary Education, the Council of Higher Secondary Education, the Central Board of Secondary Education or otherwise; college and higher education institutes, including universities; falling under government, aided, or unaided sectors, on Monday, the 20th of July, 2026,” read an order, signed by Education Commissioner-Secretary Ningthoujam Geoffrey on Sunday (July 19, 2026). 

The decision was made to let students in the football-crazy State enjoy the final without worrying about attending classes “half asleep”, a government spokesperson said.

“Most students are likely to stay awake till 3 or 4 a.m. or at least two hours from midnight and would be unable to focus on their studies a few hours later,” the spokesperson said.

Holiday in Meghalaya and Kerala too

Meghalaya Chief Minister Conrad K. Sangma has also announced a holiday for all educational institutions in the State on Monday for the World Cup final during the wee hours. “Enjoy the finals,” he said in a social media message.

The Kerala government also declared Monday a holiday for all schools and higher educational institutions in view of the FIFA World Cup final. The officials said the decision was taken after considering requests from students who are football enthusiasts and wished to watch the World Cup final on Sunday night.
