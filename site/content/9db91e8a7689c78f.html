Bullet Train corridor being equipped with advanced electrification system: NHSRCL

• THE HINDU NATIONAL • 2026-07-22

The Mumbai-Ahmedabad High Speed Rail corridor is being equipped with an advanced Overhead Traction System, one of the most sophisticated railway electrification technologies used worldwide for high-speed rail, the National High Speed Rail Corporation Limited (NHSRCL) said on Wednesday (July 22, 2026).

The advanced Overhead Traction System (2×25 kV), being introduced in India for the very first time, is specifically designed to provide a stable and uninterrupted power supply required for trains operating at very high speeds of up to 320 kmph, NHSRCL officials said. 

Electrification works are gaining pace alongside civil construction and track-laying work on the 508-km Mumbai–Ahmedabad Bullet Train corridor. 

Electricity will flow from the National Power Grid to specially designed Grid Substations and then to 14 traction substations strategically located along the corridor. “Five traction substations are located in Maharashtra—Mumbai, Thane, Virar, Boisar and Thane Depot, while the remaining nine are located in Gujarat—Vapi, Bilimora, Surat, Bharuch, Vadodara, Anand, Mahemdabad, Ahmedabad and Sabarmati Depot,” an NHSRCL statement noted.

These substations will feed power to different sections of the route, ensuring seamless transition in power supply as the Bullet Train moves from one section to another. The corridor will also have 31 switching posts to ensure uninterrupted power supply and enable rapid isolation of faults whenever required.

Apart from powering the Bullet Trains, 16 distribution substations are being constructed to supply electricity to stations, signalling systems, maintenance facilities and other operational infrastructure, the statement said. 

The trains will run on a precisely engineered network of overhead electrical wires supported by nearly 22,000 steel masts and over 25,700 cantilever assemblies spread across 1,125 track kilometres (including depots).

“Standing between 10.5 metres and 14.5 metres high—roughly the height of a three to four-storey building—these masts will support an advanced compound catenary system, another technology being deployed in India for the first time,” officials said adding, “To achieve speeds of up to 320 kmph, every wire in this system is installed under precisely calculated mechanical tension, enabling the train’s pantograph to collect electricity smoothly and continuously, without sparks, interruptions, or power fluctuations.”

Significant indigenisation

The NHSRCL stated that there has been an extensive indigenisation of critical electrical infrastructure in the bullet train project. 

Several major components of the high-speed electrification system are now being manufactured in India. “These include all major transformers, cross-arms mounted on OHE masts, as well as the ground wire, protection wire and feeder wire, which form the top electrical network of the overhead system.

In addition, a large number of components related to the Traction Power Supply System, Distribution Power System and Electro-Mechanical (E&M) works are also being manufactured domestically,” the statement noted. 

Each of the 14 traction substations will be equipped with Early Earthquake Detection Seismometers, capable of detecting seismic activity and initiating protective actions within seconds to stop trains in case of an earthquake. In total, 28 seismometers are being installed—22 along the high-speed corridor at traction substations and switching posts, and six additional inland seismometers in earthquake-prone regions of Maharashtra and Gujarat.

Of the 28 seismometers, 12 will be installed in Maharashtra near Mumbai, Thane, Virar, Boisar, Kheda, Ratnagiri, Latur and Pangri, while 16 will be located in Gujarat near Vapi, Bilimora, Surat, Bharuch, Vadodara, Anand, Mahemdabad, Ahmedabad, Adesar and Old Bhuj.

The entire electrical network will remain under real-time supervision from a centralised control centre. Automated protection systems will respond within fractions of a second to ensure safe and reliable operation of high-speed trains, the NHSRCL said.
