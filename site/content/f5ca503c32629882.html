    Don't impose conditions to avoid a debate: Rijiju tells Opposition parties

    Parliamentary Affairs Minister Kiren Rijiju, speaking after the Parliament was adjourned till noon amid Opposition din, said proceedings on day 4 were disrupted even though the government is ready for a discussion on the NEET paper ‘leak’ issue.

    “We spoke to several Opposition parties. But later they started arbitrary imposing conditions. Today, PM Modi, in very clear terms, has said that he stands by the youth of India,” Mr. Rijiju said, adding the he hopes good sense prevails on Opposition leaders.

    “This is the time to come together, to decide the future course to be taken. Parliament is the right and appropriate platform to discuss this. Don’t impose conditions to avoid a debate. It looks like they are looking for excuses,” he added.

    Govt. ready to discuss NEET issue, but Congress has put conditions: Rijiju

    As the Rajya Sabha broke into a din, Union Minister of Parliamentary Affairs Kiren Rijiju said the government had decided on a discussion on the NEET paper ‘leak’ issue after talking to all parties, but the Congress, led by Mallikarjun Kharge in the Upper House, “put conditions” on the debate.

    “Do not put conditions, this shows your intention that you don’t want to let the discussion happen,” Mr. Rijiju said.

    He also stressed on government’s intention of holding a discussion in both Houses “without politicising the matter”. Mr. Rijiju referred to Prime Minister Narendra Modi’s announcement of the government setting up fast-track courts to ensure swift and stringent punishment for those involved in paper “leaks”. It was his first direct comments tied to major protests led by the Cockroach Janta Party (CJP) in the national capita.

    “Without any conditions, I request the Chairperson to decide a time and we are ready for the discussion today itself,” he added.
