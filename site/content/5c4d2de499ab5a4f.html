Opposition continues to protest demanding a discussion on exam paper leaks and the police crackdown on student protestors; Leader of Opposition in Rajya Sabha Mallikarjun Kharge raises the issues in Rajya Sabha

• THE HINDU NATIONAL • 2026-07-22
  • lok sabha
    July 22, 2026 12:09
    Venugopal raises paper leaks issue in Lok Sabha; government ready for debate, says Rijiju; Akhilesh demands PM's statement

    Congress MP K.C. Venugopal raises the issue of question paper leaks and the police crackdown on Delhi protests. He says that Education Minister Dharmendra Pradhan must resign.

    Union Minister Kiren Rijiju replies to the remarks that the government is ready to discuss the NEET paper leak issue and the kind of discussion and the time for debate will be decided in consultation with the floor leaders of all the party.

    Samajwadi Party leader Akhilesh Yadav says that the issue is not about political parties but about the future of the youth. He says that all parties should be allowed to speak on the issue. He asks why can’t Prime Minister Narendra Modi make a statement on the students issue in Parliament if he can speak outside. 

    Meanwhile, Opposition MPs begin their protest despite Mr. Birla trying to break the logjam in the House. Mr. Birla says since Mr. Rijiju has confirmed that the government is ready for discussion, Opposition parties must come for the debate instead of protesting inside the House. However, Opposition continues their protest. The House is adjourned till 2 p.m.

    Rajya Sabha proceedings commence with Chairman C.P. Radhakrishnan in the chair. The House took up obituary references.

    Opposition MPs try to raise their demands for a discussion but the Chairman asks them to wait for their turn. 

    After taking up papers to be laid on the table, Leader of Opposition Mallikarjun Kharge raises the police crackdown and baton charge on students and the Chairman adjourns the House till 12 p.m.

    Lok Sabha adjourns minutes after convening for the day

    Proceedings commence in Lok Sabha. Opposition breaks into loud protests demanding a discussion over the issues they have given notices about. Speaker Om Birla tries to bring the House in order but to no effect. He pleads for the Opposition to allow Question Hour to continue.

    He says the House is ready to discuss any issue for any time the Opposition wants but only after the Question Hour.

    Opposition, however, continues its protest. Mr. Birla adjourns the House till 12 p.m.
