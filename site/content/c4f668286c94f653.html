'Verifying media reports' on use of pellet guns on protesters, says CRPF official

Two days after The Hindu’s report on use of pellet guns by the Rapid Action Force (RAF) against unarmed protesters during July 20 march to Parliament, a CRPF official said that they are “verifying media reports” on the issue. The official said no action taken so far, veracity is still being ascertained. 

Earlier, Delhi Police had denied the usage of pellet guns against protesters. Pellet guns and shock-batons are part of RAF’s gear, which worked alongside Delhi Police and was deployed in and around Jantar Mantar on July 20. RAF is the specialised anti-riot unit of the Central Reserve Police Force (CRPF).
