Supreme Court suggests setting up SIT to investigate donations theft at Ayodhya Ram temple

• THE HINDU NATIONAL • 2026-07-20
  • July 20, 2026 15:42
    Supreme Court suggests setting up SIT to investigate the case

    The Bench says it would read the status report and posts the case for further hearing.

    The Bench suggests setting up a SIT to investigate the case. SG Mehta agrees. 

    The court asks Mr. Mehta to consult with the Uttar Pradesh DGP about it.

    The CJI also objects to the multiplicity of the petitions in the Supreme Court.

  • July 20, 2026 15:37
    Senior advocate calls for publishing record of donations; CJI wary about false claims of donation, says inventory should be maintained

    Senior advocate Devadatt Kamat suggests that there would be records of the donations made and the records should be published so that the devotees would know. He says that devotees have given silver and gold and there are reports that these have “vanished”. He adds there should be an inventory and it should be published. 

    The CJI, however, urges for more practicality, saying that someone could falsely claim donation of diamonds, etc. But he agrees that an inventory must be maintained and accounted for.

  • July 20, 2026 15:34
    The case is a 'simple commission of an offence': CJI

    CJI Surya Kant also cautions the petitioners not to “play politics” with the case. He calls the case as a “simple commission of an offence”.

  • July 20, 2026 15:31
    CJI Surya Kant asks if SIT is investigating the case

    Chief Justice of India Surya Kant asks if there is any special investigation team (SIT) investigating the case. Solicitor General clarifies that the Uttar Pradesh Police is investigating under the oversight of the SIT and the SIT is not directly investigating.

    The CJI says, “Please have instructions to have an SIT with the senior police officer, an IG Lucknow range, who is a member of the current SIT.”

  • July 20, 2026 15:28
    Status report of the investigation filed; eight people arrested: Solicitor General

    Solicitor General Tushar Mehta, appearing for Uttar Pradesh, says the status report of the investigation has been filed. Eight people arrested. Cannot divulge more in an ongoing probe.
