    Why is the Prime Minister silent?: Rahul Gandhi on 'brutality' on students

    Leader of Opposition in Lok Sabha Rahul Gandhi on Tuesday (July 21, 2026) questioned why the Prime Minister is silent over the police crackdown during the students’ protest march led by CJP in the national capital on Monday (July 20). Mr. Gandhi also met Lok Sabha Speaker Om Birla earlier today, seeking a discussion in the House over the “brutality” on students.

    “Modi ji has not even apologised for what happened yesterday. The youth are protesting against the education and testing system. The whole country knows that the education and testing system is hollowed out by termites. Why is the Prime Minister silent? He should apologise to the students and stop this nonsense of thrashing students,” he told reporters.

    PM Modi stressed on need to punish the guilty: Rijiju on NDA Parliamentary meet

    Union Minister Kiren Rijiju, briefing the media after the NDA Parliamentary Party meet on Tuesday morning (July 21, 2026), said that PM Modi told MPs that the government took immediate action after the leak of NEET exam papers. 

    “Furthermore, prioritising the students’ futures, a re-examination was successfully conducted without delay in declaring the results... He also outlined the stringent steps being taken to avert future leaks... He stressed the need to punish the guilty and collaboratively establish a foolproof system,” Mr. Rijiju said.
